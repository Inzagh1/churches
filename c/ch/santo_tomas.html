    • “Santo Thomas de Villanueva Church”

      "Facade of Miagao Church"
      Year Built: 1786
      Located: Miag-ao, Iloilo
      Architectural Styles: Baroque Romanesque

    A royal decorated bas-relief sits in the center of Miagao church's facade, with two enormous watchtower belfries on either side. A distinctive feature of the church façade is the bas-relief, which combines influences from medieval Spanish, Chinese, Muslim, and local customs and components. The coconut tree that St. Christopher is holding onto is a key feature of the façade. It is shown as the tree of life. St. Christopher is seen carrying the Baby Jesus on his back while clad in regional attire. The remainder of the façade depicts the customs and daily activities of the inhabitants of Miagao at that time, together with local wildlife and flora (including papaya, coconut, and palm trees).
