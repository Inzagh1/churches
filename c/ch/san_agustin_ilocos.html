    • “San Agustin Church in Ilocos”

      "Paoay Church"
      Year Built: 1710
      Located: Marcos Ave. Paoay, Ilocos Norte
      Architectural Styles: Baroque Architecture

    The most notable example of earthquake baroque architecture in the Philippines is seen at Paoay Church. With its lovely baroque architecture, it transports you to the Spanish colonial era in the Philippine Islands. This magnificent national cultural treasure, which is more than three decades old, has certainly stood the test of time. The 17th century marked the beginning of Paoay Church history. What began as a drawing by Paoay Church became a reality.It was finally inaugurated in 1896. However, in 1927 and 1706, it was damage by earthquakes. Its scars just made it more attractive and worthwhile to visit. San Agustin Church Paoay was named a National Cultural Treasure by the Philippine government in 1973 and a UNESCO World Heritage Site among the nation's baroque churches in 1993 because of its historical and cultural value.
